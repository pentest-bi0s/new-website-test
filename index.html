About bi0s Pentest

India's leading cybersecurity research team, being as a part of Team bi0s.

Mission

To nurture world-class cybersecurity talent by fostering hands-on learning, research, and participation in real-world challenges like CTFs, security audits, and open-source projects.


To cultivate a culture of mentorship, integrity, and curiosity, where students not only grow as individuals but also support and uplift one another.


To bridge technical expertise with universal human values, in alignment with the vision of Amma and Amrita University, promoting both professional success and personal transformation.


To inspire a spirit of service and responsibility, by contributing to national cybersecurity awareness, developing open educational tools, and training the next generation of ethical hackers and security professionals.

Vision

To be a globally recognized, student-driven cybersecurity research and training community that empowers individuals to excel technically, grow ethically, and serve society through innovation, collaboration, and continuous learning.

Specialized Sub-Teams

Domain expertise across the security landscape

Red Teaming

On the Red Team, we simulate real-world attacks to uncover weak spots before adversaries do. It's offensive security — hands-on, high stakes, and always evolving. We think like attackers, so defenders can stay ahead.

Mobile Security

From tearing down mobile binaries to analyzing malware and exploiting flaws in mobile systems, we dig deep into how apps work — and where they fail. It's hands-on work that exposes the inner mechanics most users never see.

Blockchain Security

Decentralization doesn't guarantee safety — we prove that by uncovering logic bugs, exploitation paths, and design flaws in blockchain tech. It's fast-paced, experimental, and constantly evolving. We're deep in the code where trust meets risk.

Cloud Security

We challenge the assumptions the cloud is built on. Behind layers of abstraction lie trust boundaries waiting to be broken. We dig into the gaps others overlook — misconfigurations, flawed logic, and hidden risks — and turn them into entry points.
